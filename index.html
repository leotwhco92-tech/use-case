The Design Process

Our design process was structured to ensure we moved from understanding user needs to delivering a validated solution. Each phase built upon the last, involving continuous learning and iteration. Explore the tabs below to see our journey.

Research & Discovery

Through stakeholder interviews and owner surveys, we uncovered critical pain points. The overwhelming feedback pointed to a lack of control and excessive manual coordination. Testing & Iteration

Usability testing with five property owners revealed a key issue: the "Suspend" badge was alarming and its meaning wasn't immediately clear. Based on this feedback, we iterated on the design to improve clarity.

Before Iteration

Original "Suspend" badge was just a red icon, causing confusion.

After Iteration

Updated badge included text "Suspended" and a tooltip on hover for a clear explanation.

Outcome & Learnings

The project successfully delivered a solution that addressed the core needs of property owners, resulting in positive feedback and valuable insights for future development.

Impact

While quantitative data is pending full-scale launch, qualitative feedback from usability tests was overwhelmingly positive. Owners reported a greater sense of control and a significant reduction in perceived administrative effort. The primary success was the validation that a simple, clear status-management tool is highly desired.

Key Learnings

  • Clarity Over Cleverness: Early feedback taught us that a clear, descriptive UI is more valuable than a novel but ambiguous one.
  • Context is Crucial: Features like "Suspend" need immediate, clear context. Tooltips and descriptive text are not an afterthought.
  • Iterate Quickly: Small, rapid iterations based on user feedback led to significant improvements in usability.
