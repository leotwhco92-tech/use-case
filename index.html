        <!-- The Problem -->

        <section class="mb-12">

             <div class="card">

                <h2 class="section-title">The Problem</h2>

                <p class="section-content">

                    Property owners often face a heavy administrative burden when managing their units. The process of updating property status, coordinating with security for visitor registration, or managing tenant permissions is often manual and time-consuming. This lack of a centralized, real-time system leads to confusion, security lapses, and a poor experience for owners and tenants alike. The core problem was to create a simplified solution that allows an owner to easily update a property's status and permissions to ensure it is well-controlled without extra burden.

                </p>

            </div>

        </section>


        <!-- My Role & Responsibilities -->

        <section class="mb-12">

            <div class="card">

                <h2 class="section-title">My Role & Responsibilities</h2>

                <ul class="section-content list-disc list-inside">

                    <li>Conducted user research to understand pain points.</li>

                    <li>Designed the property card and status badges for at-a-glance information.</li>

                    <li>Created user flows for different property modes (DND, Tenant, etc.).</li>

                    <li>Developed high-fidelity prototypes for usability testing.</li>

                </ul>

            </div>

        </section>


        <!-- Target Audience -->

        <section class="mb-12">

            <div class="card">

                <h2 class="section-title">Target Audience</h2>

                <p class="section-content">

                    The primary user is the busy property owner who needs a quick, reliable way to manage single or multiple units. They require a clear overview of property status, easy delegation of control to tenants, and robust security features to prevent unauthorized access.

                </p>

            </div>

        </section>


        <!-- Scope & Constraints -->

        <section class="mb-12">

            <div class="card">

                <h2 class="section-title">Scope & Constraints</h2>

                <p class="section-content mb-4">The project's scope was focused on the core property status management features:</p>

                <ul class="section-content list-disc list-inside">

                    <li><strong>Property Card with Badges:</strong> A central UI element to display a property's status at a glance.</li>

                    <li><strong>Do Not Disturb (DND) Mode:</strong> A feature to block new visitor registrations for a specific period.</li>

                    <li><strong>Tenant Mode:</strong> A setting to transfer control of visitor and facility booking features to a tenant.</li>

                    <li><strong>Primary Property Badge:</strong> A designation for a frequently-used property to be opened by default upon app launch.</li>

                    <li><strong>Suspend Mode:</strong> A status to restrict all app features for owners who have violated rules or defaulted on payments.</li>

                </ul>

            </div>

        </section>

        

        <!-- Research & Discovery -->

        <section class="mb-12">

            <div class="card">

                <h2 class="section-title">Research & Discovery</h2>

                 <p class="section-content">

                   We used stakeholder interviews, owner surveys, and competitive analysis. A key finding was the frustration owners felt with late-night visitor calls and the lack of a simple way to delegate control to tenants. They desired a quick, visual way to see the status of their properties.

                </p>

            </div>

        </section>


        <!-- Ideation & Solution -->

        <section class="mb-12">

            <div class="card">

                <h2 class="section-title">Ideation & Solution</h2>

                <p class="section-content">

                    Based on research, we developed user flows for activating DND and Tenant modes. We decided to use prominent, colorful badges on the property card for immediate status visibility. Toggles were chosen for modes like DND and Tenant for their simplicity and clear on/off state.

                </p>

                <h2 class="section-title">Testing & Validation</h2>

                <p class="section-content">

                    Usability testing was conducted with five property owners. Initial feedback showed confusion around the "Suspend" badge. We iterated on the design by changing the color and adding a tooltip to provide a clearer explanation of the status.

                </p>

        <!-- Key Learnings & Impact -->

        <section class="mb-12">

                <h2 class="section-title">Key Learnings & Impact</h2>

                <p class="section-content">

                    This project reinforced the importance of user feedback in the design process. The new features were well-received, and owners reported feeling more in control of their properties. A key learning was to ensure that even simple UI elements like badges are self-explanatory or have clear supporting information.

                </p>
